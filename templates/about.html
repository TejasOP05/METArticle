About the Journal

The Journal of Hospital and Clinical Pharmacy Practice (JHCPP) envisions becoming a respected academic platform dedicated to advancing excellence in hospital and clinical pharmacy practice. The journal aims to foster a culture of scientific inquiry, evidence-based practice, and scholarly writing among Pharm.D students, research scholars, academicians, and clinical practitioners.

Our Vision is to:
  • Promote high-quality research and academic writing in pharmacy practice.
  • Encourage Pharm.D students to develop competencies in research methodology.
  • Highlight the evolving role of clinical pharmacists globally.
  • Contribute to the global understanding of the Pharm.D journey.
Commitment: The journal is committed to academic integrity, ethical publishing standards, and accessibility. There are no Article Processing Charges (APC). Publication is completely free of cost to support and motivate student researchers.

Instructions to Authors

  • Manuscripts must be original and not under consideration elsewhere.
  • Written in clear, concise academic English.
  • Authors must adhere to ethical research and publication practices.
  • Plagiarism (including self-plagiarism) is strictly prohibited.

A. Original Research Articles

Title Page, Structured Abstract (250–300 words), Keywords (3–6), Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion, References.

B. Review Articles

Unstructured Abstract, Introduction, Methodology (for systematic reviews), Thematic Discussion, Conclusion, References.

C. Case Reports

Abstract, Introduction, Case Presentation, Discussion, Conclusion, Patient Consent Statement, References.

  • Format: Microsoft Word (.doc, .docx)
  • Font: Times New Roman, Size 12
  • Spacing: 1.5 Line Spacing, 1 inch Margins
  • References: Vancouver style (numbered citation format)
  • Media: Tables and Figures must be numbered and editable.
Journal Policies
Ethics & Plagiarism

We adhere to international ethical standards. Plagiarism index must be ≤15% (excluding references).

Authorship Criteria

Based on substantial contributions, drafting, final approval, and accountability. Ghost authorship is prohibited.

Open Access

Authors retain copyright but grant publishing rights. No Article Processing Charges (APC).
